Fintech Mobile App

Utility Pay

A bill management app that helps users track, manage, and pay multiple utilities in one place.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Timeline

2–3 Weeks

Tools

Figma

Project Type

Personal Project

Problem Statement

Managing utility bills is often a fragmented and stressful experience. Users have to log into multiple portals with different due dates and confusing interfaces, leading to missed payments and a lack of overall expense visibility.

Key Issues Identified:

  • Portal Fatigue: Switching between multiple apps and websites to check balances
  • Payment Slippage: Lack of unified reminders leading to unintentional late fees
  • Visual Overload: Legacy portals designed for old systems, not modern users
  • Expense Fragmentation: Difficulty in understanding total monthly cost of living

Why This Matters

Reducing the cognitive burden of recurring tasks helps users maintain financial health and peace of mind through a single, trustworthy interface.

Design Goals

  • Consolidation: Bring all major utilities into a single, unified dashboard
  • Clarity: Use clean typography and high contrast to make debt and deadlines unmistakable
  • Automation: Simplify the payment flow to a single action where possible
  • Insights: Provide visual breakdowns of spending trends over time

Research & Insights

Research showed that users care most about "How much do I owe right now?" and "When is it due?". Immediate actionable data was prioritized over high-level analytics, driving the decision to place upcoming bills at the top of the experience.

Ideation & Exploration

I pivoted from complex calendars to an inbox-style card system. Each bill is treated as an item to be "cleared," providing a sense of accomplishment once paid. User flows were streamlined to ensure payments could be completed in under three taps.

Final Design

The final UI leverages a clean, high-contrast aesthetic with soft gradients to make stressful tasks feel approachable.

Expected Impact

  • Significant reduction in missed payments through unified notifications
  • 70% reduction in time spent managing multiple utility accounts
  • Better visibility into seasonal spending and usage patterns
  • Increased confidence in overall financial management

Reflections & Learnings

This project taught me the value of anticipatory design. By anticipating user anxiety around due dates, I was able to design features like the clear status pills and simplified flows that provide immediate clarity.
